Won’t I get sweaty?

That depends on how far and how quickly you cycle! Modern cycling clothes, such as sweat-wicking base layers and breathable tops really help to keep perspiration under control. Then just have a change of clothes ready at work.

Have I got to dress in Lycra and a hi-viz jacket?

Absolutely not! Baggy shorts can come with padded inserts for comfort and you can always wear Lycra shorts underneath more conservative clothes, such as trousers. In terms of hi-viz clothing, it’s always good to be as visible as possible but these days that doesn’t mean the only choice is a bright yellow jacket — you might be surprised how good modern hi-viz kit looks. Check out www.ribblecycles.co.uk to see what we mean.

Won’t I be tired before I even start work?

You’ll probably find the opposite is true. Rather than needing a couple of cups of coffee to get going, cycle commuters reach work on time — because they’re not hostage to normal commuting delays — and invigorated to start the day.

Isn’t cycling dangerous?

Department for Transport statistics for 2014 showed accidents involving cars outnumbered accidents involving cyclists at a rate of almost 10/1. Of course, more people drive than cycle, however cycling is an activity that responds to the idea of critical mass: the more people who ride, the more drivers behave responsibly, the safer our roads, leading to more people cycling. Birmingham also has hundreds of miles of cycle paths along canals or through parks so with a bit of careful route planning it’s easy to avoid busy roads.
